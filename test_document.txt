Apple Inc. is an American multinational technology company headquartered in Cupertino, California. 

The company was founded by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne on April 1, 1976. Apple's first product was the Apple I computer, designed by Wozniak.

In 2007, Steve Jobs introduced the iPhone, revolutionizing mobile computing. Tim Cook became CEO in 2011 after Jobs' passing.

Apple operates multiple divisions including:
- iPhone division, led by Craig Federighi
- Mac division, focused on personal computers
- Services division, managing cloud and digital services
- Wearables division, creating Apple Watch and AirPods

The company has offices in Palo Alto, New York, London, and Tokyo. Apple Park is their headquarters in Cupertino.

Apple's main competitors include Microsoft Corporation, Google LLC, and Samsung Electronics. The company has a market capitalization exceeding $2 trillion as of 2023.

Key financial metrics:
- Revenue: $394.3 billion (2023)
- Net income: $96.8 billion (2023)
- Employees: 161,000 worldwide

Recent products launched include iPhone 15, MacBook Pro with M3 chip, and Vision Pro.
